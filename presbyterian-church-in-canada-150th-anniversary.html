Presbyterian Church in Canada 150th Anniversary

Calling All Presbyterian Musicians! Did you know the Presbyterian Church in Canada is turning 150 in 2025? We’re planning a reception and celebration to take place at next year’s General Assembly in Hamilton, Ontario, starting June 1, and we want it to feature music and musicians from across the country. Want to be a part of the planning or perform? Contact, Jenn Geddes at revjenng@gmail.com.

Jenn Geddes and her family were long time music campers - her parents were Deans when PMC moved from Golden Lake to Tamarack! 

If you want to contribute musically, but feel like you need the support of a few like-minded people, send an email to PMC, and we’ll link you up.

Another way to participate in the PCC 150th anniversary celebrations is the PCC 150 Book. Some information...

PLEASE SEND US YOUR STORIES!  For our PCC 150 Book on COMPILED MEMORIES AND REFLECTIONS

In recognition of the PCC’s 150th Anniversary in 2025, The Committee on History is preparing a published collection of stories about where we have been, where we are and where we are going.

Do you have a favourite memory of singing in a choir, attending a youth group, going to a potluck supper, or attending the General Assembly—or anything else that shaped your involvement in church? What are some of the things your congregation is doing today to share the good news of Jesus in your community? And what are your hopes and dreams for the PCC as it approaches 150 years? We want to hear from you! Please write to Ian McKechnie, project coordinator, at pcc150book@gmail.com

Submissions are being accepted until November 30, 2024.
