Committee Meeting Summary - September 2019

We are trying something new this year! After each committee meeting, we’ll send a brief summary to our camp community so you can see what we’re up to.

We had our first committee meeting on September 28. We welcomed several new PMC Committee members, and said thanks and farewell to a few others. Special thanks to our outgoing Deans, Sue Thompson and Kim Lovatt, and to our outgoing Convener Ruth Anne Robertson, as they transition to other (just as demanding) roles in their lives.

Mark your calendars!! Camp in 2020 will be August 23-30.

Welcome to David Linderman and Hugh Donnelly as Deans for 2020 and 2021. They have some great theme ideas – we’ll update you soon.

The PMC Committee for 2019-2020 is:
Conveners: Taras Broadhead and Cynthia McMinn
Treasurer: Debi Vanderstelt
Secretary: Alison Cooper
Registrar: Cathy Whiteside
Merchandise: Cathy Whiteside
Staffing: Jesse Fegelman and Kim Lovatt
Advertising: TBC
Website: Daniel Eisner
Camp Liaison: Anne Audet
Teen Rep: Lisa Broadhead
Cairn Reps: MJ Perry, Doug Kendall

Among the many things we talked about at the meeting were these highlights:

  • Planning is underway for new promotional videos for PMC, to use for introducing PMC to new campers. Sarah Dupont is working on scripts.
  • An improved agreement for sharing the Tamarack site with Camp Tournesol (the French camp); we are working on a plan to implement an improved agreement in 2020.
  • The future and role of Coffee House; we will consider this further as we plan for 2020.
  • We have the banner to fix over the winter (repairing the Great Mousecapade); Ian MacCready has the parts to fix the banner stand.

Want to make a joyful noise over the winter? It’s not too late to get a djembe to practice on your own, or to add “grace notes” to your Sunday service – contact MJ Perry or any committee member for information.

Look for our next update in early December for more information on these and other topics, including a possible teen retreat in the New Year. AND watch for a picture in Presbyterian Connection (check the PCC website for details).
